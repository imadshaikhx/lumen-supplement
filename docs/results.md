These findings are preliminary. They provide early validity evidence, not clinical validation.

## Sample

- N = 152 recruited.
- N = 144 retained after incomplete and attention-check exclusions.
- N = 122 primary analytic sample after careless-response exclusion.

## Reliability and structure

| Level | k | N | α | ω | PC1 variance | PC2 variance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Grandiose (GF, SSSE, EXP) | 3 | 121 | .09 | .35 | 41.1% | 33.2% |
| Vulnerable (ER, CSE, HS, DEV) | 4 | 121 | .39 | .51 | 37.3% | 26.5% |
| Total (all 7 dimensions) | 7 | 120 | .28 | .40 | 21.7% | 18.9% |

Reliability was modest overall. Parallel analysis retained two components, which supports separating grandiose and vulnerable scores more than treating the seven dimensions as one strong total scale.

## Incremental validity

Hierarchical regressions tested whether Lumen composites added prediction beyond Dirty Dozen narcissism self-report.

| Outcome | Lumen score | ΔR² | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Perceived stress | Vulnerable composite | .041 | .024 |
| Misconduct | Vulnerable composite | .034 | .040 |
| Depression | Vulnerable composite | .047 | .016 |

The effects were modest. They suggest that vulnerable narrative scores carried outcome-relevant information not captured by the brief Dirty Dozen narcissism comparison.

## Confidence effects

Each score included a model confidence tier. Confidence analyses were exploratory.

For Entitlement Rage and life satisfaction:

- All cases: r = −.27, N = 122.
- Medium and high confidence only: r = −.38, N = 73.
- High confidence only: r = −.43, N = 48.

This pattern suggests confidence-weighted scoring is worth testing in Study 2. It does not prove that model confidence is a validated quality metric.

## Limitations

- The sample was non-clinical and online.
- Direct PNI self-report was not collected.
- Reliability was modest.
- Hiding the Self showed a ceiling effect.
- Exploitativeness scoring needs revision.
- Confidence moderation was exploratory.

Study 2 will revise scoring, add direct PNI comparison, and test whether confidence-weighted scoring improves prediction.

* p < .05. ** p < .01.
