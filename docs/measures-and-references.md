# Measures and References

[Home](../index.html) | [Method](method.md) | [Scoring](scoring.md) | [Dimensions](dimensions.md) | [Results](results.md) | [Data availability](data-availability.md)

Study 1 used the following self-report measures after the Lumen interview.

| Measure | Construct | Role in Study 1 | Citation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Satisfaction With Life Scale | Life satisfaction | Outcome | Diener et al., 1985 |
| Perceived Stress Scale | Perceived stress | Outcome | Cohen et al., 1983 |
| PHQ-9 | Depression severity | Outcome | Kroenke et al., 2001 |
| Counterproductive Work Behavior scale | Workplace misconduct | Outcome | Bennett and Robinson, 2000 |
| Organizational Citizenship Behavior scale | Prosocial work behavior | Outcome | Lee and Allen, 2002 |
| Michigan Organizational Assessment Questionnaire | Job satisfaction | Outcome | Cammann et al., 1983 |
| Dirty Dozen | Dark Triad traits | Self-report comparison | Jonason and Webster, 2010 |
| Pathological Narcissism Inventory | Conceptual basis for scored dimensions | Conceptual basis | Pincus et al., 2009 |

The Pathological Narcissism Inventory was the conceptual basis for the Lumen dimensions. It was not administered as a self-report measure in Study 1.
