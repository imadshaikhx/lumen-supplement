This page gives a public scoring summary. Exact prompt wording and detailed anchors are not posted because the interview may be revised and reused in future studies.

## Purpose

Lumen turns open-ended interview responses into research scores for seven narcissism-relevant dimensions inspired by the Pathological Narcissism Inventory.

Scores are used for group-level research analyses. They are not clinical classifications.

## Score scale

| Score | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | No or minimal evidence of the dimension |
| 2 | Weak or inconsistent evidence |
| 3 | Moderate evidence |
| 4 | Strong evidence |

## Confidence tier

| Tier | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| High | The response clearly supports the assigned score |
| Medium | The response contains usable but mixed or limited evidence |
| Low | The response is vague, brief, off-topic, or difficult to score |

## General scoring principles

- Score what is present in the response, not an assumed hidden motive.
- Do not treat ordinary ambition, privacy, disappointment, or self-advocacy as enough evidence by itself.
- Higher scores require clearer evidence of the target dimension.
- Lower confidence is appropriate when responses are short, vague, mixed, or only indirectly relevant.
- Scores summarize one research response. They do not diagnose a person.

## Study 1 scoring notes

Entitlement Rage showed the clearest item-level result in Study 1. Hiding the Self showed ceiling compression, which limited variation. Exploitativeness needs rubric revision to better separate ordinary networking from exploitative interpersonal strategy.

Study 2 priorities include direct PNI comparison, revised score anchors, improved Hiding the Self variation, and stronger tests of confidence-weighted scoring.
