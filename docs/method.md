## Sample

Study 1 recruited 152 adults through Prolific. Two incomplete records were removed. Six participants who failed two or more attention checks were excluded, leaving 144 retained participants.

The primary analytic sample was N = 122 after excluding 22 participants with a careless-response index of 2 or higher.

## Procedure

Participants completed the Lumen interview before self-report questionnaires. The interview-first order reduced the chance that questionnaire items would shape open-ended responses.

The interview used seven open-ended behavioral prompts. Each prompt corresponded to one dimension inspired by the Pathological Narcissism Inventory.

## Scoring

Each response was scored from 1 to 4 on the target dimension. Higher scores indicate clearer evidence of the focal dimension in that response. Each score also received a high, medium, or low confidence tier.

Confidence tiers were used for exploratory analyses. They should not be treated as validated quality ratings.

## Measures

Self-report measures followed the interview. Study 1 included measures of life satisfaction, perceived stress, depression symptoms, workplace misconduct, organizational citizenship behavior, job satisfaction, and Dark Triad traits.

The Dirty Dozen narcissism subscale was the available self-report narcissism comparison. Direct Pathological Narcissism Inventory self-report was not collected in Study 1.

## Analyses

Analyses examined reliability, score structure, Dirty Dozen correlations, criterion associations, incremental prediction beyond Dirty Dozen narcissism, and exploratory confidence effects. Most reported results use the primary analytic sample of N = 122.
