# LUMEN

**An AI-delivered behavioral interview for pathological narcissism**

Imad Shaikh, Nikolai Ortiz, Muchen Xi, and Tianjun Sun  
Rice University SMART Lab

Lumen tests whether open-ended interview responses can be scored for narcissism-relevant patterns. Study 1 examined whether AI-scored narrative responses showed early validity evidence, with special attention to vulnerable narcissism and outcome associations beyond traditional self-report.

Lumen is a research tool, not a diagnostic tool.

## Study 1

Participants completed the Lumen interview before self-report measures. The primary analytic sample included 122 participants after exclusions and careless-response screening.

Study 1 examined reliability, structure, convergent and discriminant patterns using the Dirty Dozen, criterion associations, incremental prediction, and exploratory confidence effects.

Direct convergence with the Pathological Narcissism Inventory was not tested in Study 1.

## Main results

- Reliability was modest overall.
- Vulnerable scores showed the clearest outcome associations.
- Entitlement Rage was associated with lower life satisfaction, r = −.27, 95% CI [−.43, −.09], p = .003.
- The vulnerable composite added modest prediction beyond Dirty Dozen narcissism self-report for stress, misconduct, and depression.
