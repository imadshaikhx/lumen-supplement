Rice University SMART Lab

LUMEN

An AI-delivered behavioral interview for pathological narcissism

Study 1 tested whether narrative interview responses could provide early validity evidence for narcissism-relevant scores beyond traditional self-report.

Imad Shaikh, Nikolai Ortiz, Muchen Xi, and Tianjun Sun

What Lumen Does

Lumen asks people to describe real experiences, then scores those narratives for patterns related to pathological narcissism.

Participants answer seven open-ended behavioral prompts. Each prompt maps to a dimension inspired by the Pathological Narcissism Inventory, or PNI. Responses are scored from 1 to 4, with a high, medium, or low confidence tier. Scores are used for research analyses, not clinical classification.

The Lumen interview came before self-report questionnaires. The Dirty Dozen was the available Dark Triad self-report comparison. Direct PNI self-report was not collected in Study 1.

Main Results

  • Reliability was modest overall: grandiose α = .09, ω = .35; vulnerable α = .39, ω = .51; total α = .28, ω = .40.
  • Parallel analysis retained two components.
  • Vulnerable scores showed the clearest outcome associations.
  • Entitlement Rage was associated with lower life satisfaction, r = −.27, 95% CI [−.43, −.09], p = .003.
  • The vulnerable composite added modest prediction beyond Dirty Dozen narcissism self-report for stress, misconduct, and depression.

Incremental validity

Vulnerable composite results beyond Dirty Dozen narcissism self-report:

  • Stress ΔR² = .041, p = .024.
  • Misconduct ΔR² = .034, p = .040.
  • Depression ΔR² = .047, p = .016.

What This Means

Study 1 suggests that narrative interview responses may carry outcome-relevant information about vulnerable narcissism beyond brief self-report narcissism measures. These findings are preliminary and should be interpreted as early validity evidence, not clinical validation.

Limitations and Next Steps

  • Study 1 used a non-clinical online sample.
  • Direct PNI self-report was not collected.
  • Reliability was modest.
  • Hiding the Self showed a ceiling effect.
  • Exploitativeness scoring needs revision.
  • Confidence moderation was exploratory.

Study 2 will revise scoring and add direct PNI comparison.
